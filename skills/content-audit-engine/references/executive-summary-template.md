# Executive Summary Template

## Document Overview

**File name:** `[ClientDomain]_Content-Audit_[YYYY-MM].docx`
**Maximum length:** 12 pages
**Tone:** Professional, direct, data-backed. No filler. Every sentence earns its place.
**Audience:** Decision-makers — marketing directors, CMOs, agency clients. Not SEO specialists.

---

## Page-by-Page Structure

### Page 1 — Cover Page

Layout:
```
[CLIENT DOMAIN or COMPANY NAME]
Content Audit — Executive Summary

Prepared: [Month YYYY]
Prepared by: [Agency or "Internal"]
Data period: [Start Date] – [End Date]

[Optional: client logo or minimal design element — no clipart]
```

No prose on the cover page. Clean, professional, one glance to understand what this document is.

## Formatting Rules

### General Rules

- Use tables for any comparison with 3+ items
- Use bullet points (not paragraphs) for lists of 4+ observations
- Lead with the number, then the insight: "39% of pages (136) are dead weight" — not "The site has quite a lot of pages that don't get traffic"
- Every section ends with an actionable implication — not just "here's what we found" but "here's what it means"

### Callout Boxes

Use 3–5 callout boxes throughout the document for the most important findings:

Format:
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  KEY FINDING                                        │
│  39% of pages are dead weight — zero traffic,       │
│  no rankings, consuming crawl budget. Recommend     │
│  pruning or consolidating 136 pages.                │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### What NOT to Include

- Generic advice ("create more content", "write longer posts", "optimize for keywords")
- Observations without data backing
- Competitor metrics presented as facts (always use "approximately" or "estimated")
- SEO jargon without explanation if audience is non-technical
- More than 12 pages total — cut aggressively if needed

### Page Count Management

If analysis generates more content than fits in 12 pages:
1. Cut the per-keyword SERP detail — keep cross-keyword summary only
2. Cut the bottom-10 pages table — summarize as callout stat instead
3. Reduce competitor profiles from full paragraphs to bullet points
4. Remove secondary opportunities — keep top 10 instead of top 15
5. Never cut: executive findings, opportunity list, recommended experiments
